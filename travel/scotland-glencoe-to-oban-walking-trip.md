# Scotland Walking Trip: Glencoe to Oban

## Trip Overview
- **Duration**: 9 days
- **Daily Distance**: Approximately 10 miles (16 km) per day
- **Start Point**: Glencoe
- **End Point**: Oban
- **Total Distance**: ~90 miles
- **Bag Transfer Service**: Recommended (see details below)

## Recommended Bag Transfer Companies
- **Sherpa Van Project**: www.sherpavan.com
- **Baggage Transfer Company**: www.baggagetransfercompany.co.uk
- **AMS Baggage Transfer**: www.amsbaggage.co.uk

These services will transport your luggage between B&Bs while you walk unencumbered.

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1: Glencoe to Kinlochleven (10 miles)
**Accommodation**: Kinlochleven B&B options
- Allt-na-Leven House
- Edencoille Guest House
- Macdonald Hotel

**Route Description**:
- Start at Glencoe Village
- Follow the West Highland Way through Devil's Staircase
- Stunning views of the Mamores and Ben Nevis
- Descend into Kinlochleven

**Highlights**:
- Devil's Staircase climb (550m ascent)
- Panoramic mountain views
- Historic aluminum smelting village

**Terrain**: Moderate to challenging with significant ascent

---

### Day 2: Kinlochleven to Fort William (14 miles - slightly longer)
**Accommodation**: Fort William B&Bs
- Glenlochy Nevis Bridge
- The Grange B&B
- Cruachan House

**Route Description**:
- Follow West Highland Way northward
- Pass Lairigmor
- Descend towards Glen Nevis
- Enter Fort William at the base of Ben Nevis

**Highlights**:
- Views of Britain's highest mountain
- Nevis Gorge
- Outdoor capital atmosphere

**Terrain**: Moderate with varied elevation

---

### Day 3: Fort William to Gairlochy (10 miles)
**Accommodation**: Gairlochy area B&Bs
- Gairlochy Holiday Park
- Local farm B&Bs (book in advance)

**Route Description**:
- Follow the Great Glen Way
- Walk alongside Caledonian Canal
- Pass Neptune's Staircase lock system

**Highlights**:
- Thomas Telford's engineering marvel
- Canal towpath walking
- Peaceful lochside scenery

**Terrain**: Easy, mostly flat canal paths

---

### Day 4: Gairlochy to Spean Bridge (9 miles)
**Accommodation**: Spean Bridge B&Bs
- Corriegour Lodge Hotel
- Distant Hills Guest House
- Riverside B&B

**Route Description**:
- Continue on Great Glen Way
- Mix of forestry tracks and minor roads
- Views of Loch Lochy

**Highlights**:
- Commando Memorial
- World War II history
- Highland scenery

**Terrain**: Easy to moderate

---

### Day 5: Spean Bridge to Roybridge and Glen Spean (11 miles)
**Accommodation**: Roybridge area
- Grey Corrie Lodge
- Aonach Mor Guest House

**Route Description**:
- Head southwest through Glen Spean
- Forestry paths and quiet roads
- Begin transitioning toward Loch Ossian area

**Highlights**:
- Remote Highland glen
- Mountain panoramas
- Traditional crofting landscape

**Terrain**: Moderate

---

### Day 6: Roybridge to Rannoch Station via Loch Ossian (16 miles - longer day or split)
**Accommodation**: 
- Loch Ossian Youth Hostel (remote, no road access)
- OR continue to Rannoch Station Hotel

**Alternative**: This can be split into two shorter days

**Route Description**:
- Follow old drove roads and tracks
- Cross remote moorland
- One of the most isolated sections

**Highlights**:
- Complete wilderness experience
- Rannoch Moor views
- Remote mountain lochs

**Terrain**: Challenging, remote terrain

---

### Day 7: Rannoch Station to Bridge of Orchy (10 miles)
**Accommodation**: Bridge of Orchy
- Bridge of Orchy Hotel
- Inveroran Hotel (nearby)

**Route Description**:
- Join West Highland Way again
- Cross Rannoch Moor
- Scenic moorland and mountain walking

**Highlights**:
- Rannoch Moor landscape
- Ba Bridge
- Classic Highland scenery

**Terrain**: Moderate

---

### Day 8: Bridge of Orchy to Tyndrum (7 miles, short day)
**Accommodation**: Tyndrum B&Bs
- Glengarry Guest House
- Dalkell B&B
- By The Way Hostel

**Route Description**:
- Continue on West Highland Way
- Pass beneath Beinn Dorain
- Arrive at historic gold-panning village

**Highlights**:
- Mountain scenery
- Rest day opportunity
- Real Food Café (famous stop)

**Terrain**: Moderate

---

### Day 9: Tyndrum to Dalmally to Oban (Split with transport)
**Final Day Options**:

**Option A**: Walk Tyndrum to Dalmally (12 miles), then bus/train to Oban
- Walk alongside Loch Awe
- Kilchurn Castle views
- Public transport to Oban (bus or train available)

**Option B**: Taxi to Connel, walk final 5 miles into Oban
- Coastal walking
- Connel Bridge views
- Triumphant arrival in Oban

**Accommodation in Oban**:
- Roseneath Guest House
- Glenburnie House
- Hawthornbank Guest House

**Highlights**:
- Oban Bay views
- Seafood capital of Scotland
- McCaig's Tower
- Ferry port atmosphere

---

## Essential Information

### Best Time to Visit
- **May to September**: Best weather, longer days
- **June-August**: Peak season, book accommodations early
- **September**: Fewer midges, autumn colors

### What to Pack
- Waterproof jacket and trousers
- Walking boots (broken in)
- Warm layers
- Sun protection
- Water bottles (2L capacity)
- Snacks and lunch supplies
- First aid kit
- Map and compass/GPS
- Midge repellent (summer months)

### Difficulty Level
- **Overall**: Moderate to Challenging
- **Fitness Required**: Good general fitness
- **Experience**: Some hill walking experience recommended
- **Navigation**: Well-marked trails but map skills useful

### Booking Bag Transfer
Contact bag transfer companies at least 2-4 weeks in advance. Provide:
- Itinerary with B&B addresses
- Number of bags
- Special requirements

### Food and Supplies
- Fort William: Full resupply point
- Other villages: Limited shops, plan ahead
- Carry lunch daily
- B&Bs often provide packed lunches (arrange evening before)

### Emergency Contacts
- Emergency Services: 999 or 112
- Mountain Rescue: via 999
- NHS 24: 111

### Alternative Routes
If weather is poor or you need easier options:
- Use public transport to skip challenging sections
- Road alternatives available for most days
- Flexibility is key in Highland weather

### Public Transport Links
- **To Glencoe**: Bus from Glasgow or Fort William
- **From Oban**: Trains to Glasgow; ferries to islands
- **Intermediate**: Limited bus services; plan carefully

## Estimated Costs
- **Bag Transfer**: £10-15 per bag per day (£90-135 total)
- **B&B Accommodation**: £35-60 per person per night
- **Food**: £25-35 per day
- **Total Estimated**: £700-1000 per person for 9 days

## Additional Notes
This itinerary provides a balanced journey from the dramatic mountains of Glencoe to the coastal charm of Oban. The route combines sections of the West Highland Way with quieter paths through the Highlands. Daily distances average 10 miles but can be adjusted based on fitness and weather conditions.

Consider building in rest days or flexibility for poor weather, particularly in the more remote sections around Rannoch Moor.
